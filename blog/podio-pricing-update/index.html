    Navigating Podio’s New Pricing Changes: What You Need to Know (and What to Do Next)

    For years, Podio has been a reliable workhorse for businesses managing complex workflows, largely thanks to its generous Premium tier that included a massive 5 million automation actions per month. However, a recent shift in Podio’s pricing model is leaving many power users with unexpected choices to make.

    Podio is rolling out new limits effective August 1st, reducing the included automation actions for Premium users from 5 million down to just 25,000 per month. For organizations that rely heavily on Podio's automation engine to run their daily operations, hitting this new ceiling will happen fast.

    Here is a breakdown of the recent changes, what they mean for your bottom line, and the options you have moving forward.

    The New Reality: Automation and API Add-ons

    If your organization exceeds the new 25,000 monthly action limit when the new pricing goes live on August 1st, you will be required to purchase an Automation Add-on to keep your workflows running.

    Based on the newly released pricing tiers, scaling back up to the capacity you previously enjoyed will come at a premium:

    • 100,000 actions/month: $39/month
    • 500,000 actions/month: $119/month
    • 1,000,000 actions/month: $159/month
    • 5,000,000 actions/month: $559/month

    In addition to automation actions, Podio is also introducing tiered API add-ons for businesses with higher integration needs, starting at $23/month for 2,500 calls per hour, and scaling up to $159/month for 50,000 calls per hour.

    For heavy users, maintaining your current infrastructure on Podio could mean a significant increase in your monthly SaaS overhead.

    Your 3 Best Options Moving Forward

    If you are an affected user wondering how to handle these changes, you have three primary paths to consider.

    1. Accept the New Pricing and Purchase an Add-On

    If migrating your systems or rebuilding your workflows isn't feasible right now, the simplest solution is to bite the bullet. Once the new pricing takes effect on August 1st, you can evaluate your monthly usage and purchase the corresponding Automation or API add-on. While this increases your monthly costs, it ensures zero disruption to your current operations.

    2. Optimize Your Workflows to Reduce Actions

    A surprisingly high number of automation actions are wasted on inefficient workflows. Redundant triggers, endless loops, and poorly structured flows can quickly drain your 25,000 action limit.

    Instead of paying for higher tiers, you can work with a certified Podio partner like Zooli. We can audit your workspace, consolidate your flows, and optimize your systems to dramatically reduce the number of actions your business consumes each month.

    3. Migrate to a Modern Alternative Like Tape

    If you love the way Podio organizes data, you will feel right at home. Tape is structured exactly like Podio, utilizing the same intuitive concept of workspaces and apps to map out your business. There is no confusing paradigm shift or steep learning curve - just a faster, cleaner version of what you already know.

    But Tape isn't just a replacement; it’s a massive upgrade. While keeping the core structure familiar, Tape delivers advanced features that Podio users have been requesting for a decade:

    • Native Dashboards: Build powerful, real-time visual charts and reports right inside your workspace without relying on clunky third-party add-ons.
    • Granular Access Control: Gain superior, precise security over who can view, edit, or interact with specific fields and records.
    • Customizable App Layouts: Break out of Podio's rigid single-column structure and design a workspace tailored exactly to how your team works.
